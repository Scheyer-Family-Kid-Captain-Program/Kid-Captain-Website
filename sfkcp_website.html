The Program

A Game Day Moment That Lasts a Lifetime

Selected pediatric patients at Duke Children's Hospital are honored as honorary Kid Captains at Duke Men's Basketball games. They walk onto the Cameron Indoor Stadium court, meet the players, hear their name in front of thousands of fans, and are celebrated as champions.

The program is powered by Holly and the Child Life Specialist team — advocates who walk with each child and family from arrival to departure. Each Kid Captain's experience is personalized: connecting the child's interests with a player's story, creating a memory unlike any other.

Currently, 7–8 children are honored per season. Our mission is to dramatically expand that reach — to more kids, for longer, and through more Duke sports than just basketball.

Our Objective: Scale Sustainably, Reach Further

Expand the Scheyer Family Kid Captain Program by improving reach, deepening community partnerships in Durham, and creating year-round programming that keeps every child connected — not just on game day.

Expanding Impact

Reaching More Kids, For Longer

The game day moment is just the beginning. We're building year-round programming so every child at Duke Children's Hospital feels seen, celebrated, and connected to the Duke community.

📺

Virtual Inclusion

Children who can't physically attend games receive personalized video messages from players, are acknowledged during broadcasts, and are first in line for the following season's Kid Captain selection.

🏈

All Duke Sports

Expanding beyond basketball to Duke Football (PT sessions), Women's Basketball, Lacrosse, and more. Every team can bring joy to the hospital — and we're making it easy for them to do so.

📦

Care Packages

Kids who miss game day receive Duke Athletics care packages with signed memorabilia, team gear, and handwritten notes from players. No child is left uncelebrated.

⛺

Summer Camp & Field Days

A "Scheyer Family Kid Captain Week" at Scheyer's basketball camp for kids who can afford it — with scholarship options for those who can't. Field days and outdoor activities on the hospital grounds for all.

🏥

Children's Health Center

Expanding into the outpatient Children's Health Center (CHP) so that kids receiving ongoing care — not just inpatients — can participate in programming and feel part of the Duke family.

🤝

Durham Community Partnerships

Partnering with the Boys & Girls Club, YMCA, Emily K Center, and local Durham organizations to bring programming beyond the hospital walls and into the broader community.

Get Involved

Your Path to Making a Difference

You don't need to be a Duke athlete to change a child's day. Anyone can become a Kid Captain volunteer — it takes a 2-hour training and a heart for kids who are fighting hard battles.

1

Fill Out the Interest Form

Takes 2 minutes. Tell us a little about yourself and your interest in the program.

2

Holly Reaches Out

Holly (Child Life Specialist) or the volunteer coordinator will contact you within a week.

3

Complete 2-Hour Training

Offered in summer, fall, and winter. Learn hospital protocols, patient safety, and how to be a great volunteer.

4

6-Month Commitment

Join Monday playroom sessions at the hospital. Regular, consistent presence means everything to the kids.

5

Join Special Events

Participate in hospital visits, game day preparation, care package creation, and community field days.

Duke Student Athletes

Fast-Track Access

Duke student athletes have a direct pathway to visit patients and participate in hospital events. Coordinate through your team's staff and the Duke Athletics volunteer manager. All sports welcome — not just basketball.

Duke Students & Durham Community

Community Volunteer Track

Duke students and Durham community members are welcome! Complete the volunteer training (offered 3x per year), commit to 6 months of Monday sessions, and you'll be part of a growing movement to reach more kids.
